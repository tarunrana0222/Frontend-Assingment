Revolution

A grocery revolution began in earnest in 2017, and itï¿½s far from over. An industry that was the stable, boring bedrock of the consumer market for decades came under pressure from all directions this past year. The moats which had protected this reliable business were drained by new technologies, growing consumer revolt from our current unsustainable food system, and a looming logistics revolution on the horizon. The old model is dying. To survive, grocers need to deliver fresher, healthier, more convenient, transparent food, and cater to local markets and demographics. They must engage the customer with exciting new flavors, ethnic specialties, dietary specific foods, sustainably and ethically raised as well as locally grown food while making it more convenient, nutritious, and instructional. They need laser like focus and targeted solutions to curate a personal experience that is exciting enough to entice a store visit, creating stores that are organoleptically pleasing, easy and expedient to get the shopper home.
